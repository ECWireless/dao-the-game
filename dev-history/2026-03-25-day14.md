# Day 14

## Summary

Turned the demo into a much more legible, player-readable management game: clearer worker identities, a stronger Factory flow, better recovery and inspection UX, faster artifact generation, and chain selection that now cleanly defaults to Sepolia in local dev and Base in production.

## Key Changes

- Refined the active worker roster down to six clear hires: two frontend engineers, two designers, and two review/release specialists, with much stronger PM-facing distinctions and cleaner user-facing profile info.
- Simplified the RaidGuild and Whiteboard hiring surfaces so workers pitch themselves briefly in-world, avatars open the full profile, and importing/reassigning workers is more obvious.
- Renamed the deployment app from `Machine` to `Factory`, updated the iconography, and cleaned up the app shell/notification flow to match the new framing.
- Improved the Factory interaction model so players can inspect the client brief, click completed worker nodes to see short reports addressed to their studio, close the final output to return to the assembly view, and recover cleanly after interrupted assembly.
- Reworked the artifact-generation pipeline for speed: lightweight design/deployment passes, implementation as the sole HTML builder, review as a judge-only QA pass, compact stage contexts, timing logs, and a quiet retry path for flaky review judgments.
- Added coarse “usual duration” hints to Factory nodes so players have a better sense of where time is going during assembly.
